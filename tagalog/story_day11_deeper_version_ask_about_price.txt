[NARRATOR]: Translated

[TAGALOG-MALE-1]: Manong, magkano po hanggang sa Nacpan Beach?
[NARRATOR]: Brother driver, how much to Nacpan Beach?

[TAGALOG-MALE-2]: Isandaang piso po bawat tao, sir.
[NARRATOR]: One hundred pesos per person, sir.

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-1]: Saan po kami makakakuha ng tricycle pabalik?
[NARRATOR]: Where can we get a tricycle back? Note: "makakakuha" implies finding or obtaining transportation.

[TAGALOG-MALE-2]: Doon po sa tabi ng sari-sari store. Maraming naghihintay doon.
[NARRATOR]: Over there beside the convenience store. Many are waiting there.

[TAGALOG-MALE-1]: Ah, salamat po. Kailangan po ba naming magbayad ngayon?
[NARRATOR]: Ah, thank you. Do we need to pay now?

[TAGALOG-MALE-2]: Hindi pa po. Pagdating po namin doon, tsaka po kayo magbabayad.
[NARRATOR]: Not yet. When we arrive there, then you'll pay. Note: This shows trust-based payment common in Filipino culture.

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-1]: Manong, may mga ibang tourist spots po ba na malapit sa Nacpan?
[NARRATOR]: Brother, are there other tourist spots near Nacpan?

[TAGALOG-MALE-2]: Oo, meron pong Calitang Beach. Limampung piso lang po dagdag kung gusto ninyo.
[NARRATOR]: Yes, there's Calitang Beach. Just fifty pesos extra if you want.

[TAGALOG-MALE-1]: Magkano po lahat kung dalawang beach?
[NARRATOR]: How much total for two beaches?

[TAGALOG-MALE-2]: Isandaan singkuwenta po bawat tao. Sulit na po yan.
[NARRATOR]: One hundred fifty per person. That's worth it already. Note: "Sulit na" expresses good value.

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-2]: Ate, ano pong mga ulam ninyo ngayon?
[NARRATOR]: Sister, what dishes do you have today?

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-1]: Meron pong inihaw na bangus, adobong manok, at sinigang na hipon.
[NARRATOR]: We have grilled milkfish, chicken adobo, and shrimp sinigang.

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-2]: Masarap po ba ang sinigang ninyo?
[NARRATOR]: Is your sinigang delicious?

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-1]: Oo naman! Sariwang hipon po yan, kakahuli lang ngayong umaga.
[NARRATOR]: Of course! Those are fresh shrimp, just caught this morning. Note: "Kakahuli lang" emphasizes very recent freshness.

[TAGALOG-MALE-1]: Magkano po ang presyo nito?
[NARRATOR]: How much is the price of this?

[TAGALOG-FEMALE-1]: Dalawandaan po with rice. Ano pa pong specialty namin? Meron din pong kinilaw na tanigue.
[NARRATOR]: Two hundred with rice. What other specialty do we have? We also have tanigue kinilaw. Note: Code-switching with "with rice" is natural in Filipino English.
